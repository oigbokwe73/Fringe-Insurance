# Use Case: Codex with Xenhey for Outcome-Based Insurance Solutions

## Client: Fringe Insurance

## Insurance Lines: Car Insurance, Home Insurance, Business Insurance

## 1. Executive Summary

Fringe Insurance wants a faster and more flexible way to design, present, validate, and launch digital insurance experiences across its car, home, and business insurance lines.

Today, sales, product, marketing, and technology teams often lose time moving between requirements documents, UI mockups, developers, sample data files, API payloads, approval cycles, and manual revisions. This slows down feedback and makes it harder to quickly show business stakeholders what the final customer experience will look like.

**Codex with Xenhey** provides an outcome-based solution that allows Fringe Insurance to rapidly create interactive HTML experiences, edit content inline, generate sample JSON data, validate forms and tables, and demonstrate working insurance workflows in real time.

Xenhey becomes the fast feedback and publishing layer, while Codex acts as the solution-building assistant that generates structured HTML, form logic, table layouts, sample data, payloads, and workflow steps.

The result is a faster path from business conversation to visible working prototype.

## 3. Problem Statement

Fringe Insurance currently faces several common challenges:

### Slow Digital Experience Delivery

Building new insurance forms, customer portals, landing pages, and quote workflows requires back-and-forth between business teams and development teams.

### Limited Real-Time Feedback

Sales and business stakeholders cannot easily change text, form fields, or table layouts during meetings without waiting for development updates.

### Manual Sample Data Creation

Teams need realistic sample data for car, home, and business insurance forms, but creating this data manually is time-consuming and inconsistent.

### Disconnected Requirements and UI

Business requirements are often captured in documents, while the UI and API payloads are created separately. This causes missed fields, inconsistent naming, and rework.

### Difficulty Demonstrating Outcomes

Sales teams need to show outcomes quickly, not just talk about technical capability. Fringe needs to see what the customer and agent experience will look like.
